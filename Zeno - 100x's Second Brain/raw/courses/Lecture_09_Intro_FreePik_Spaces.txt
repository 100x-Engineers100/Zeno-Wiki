SECTION 1 — WHAT IS FREEPIK?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Pre-AI era: Stock image/video/illustration library.
Post-AI era: Repositioned as an AI aggregator.

What's an AI aggregator?
A platform that takes the best AI models, pulls their APIs,
and wraps them in a polished UI. Users don't need to manage
models directly — they just choose and generate.

Models currently available on FreePik:
- Google Imagen 3 (NanaBanana)
- Google Imagen 3 Pro
- Google Imagen 2 (fast)
- CDance 2.0 (just launched during this lecture week)
- Flux 2.2 Pro
- CDream 5 Lite
- Kling 2.5 / 3.0 (video)
- LTX 2 (video)
- OpenAI Sora (until April 24th)

Other aggregators like FreePik: Higgsfield, OpenArt, Genspark.
FreePik chosen specifically because it has FreePik Spaces
(node-based workflow interface — covered in Lecture 10).

Why your ComfyUI knowledge gives you an edge here:
Most users on FreePik just prompt and click.
They don't understand: latent space, ControlNet, IP adapters,
referencing, LoRA behavior. You do.
That's where your leverage is — you can extract better results
because you understand what's happening under the hood.

Analogy from Pranay:
"FreePik is like learning Framer (frontend builder with templates).
ComfyUI was learning React/Next.js from scratch."
Both valid — different levels of control.

SECTION 2 — FREEPIK UI NAVIGATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Three things you need (pinned in left sidebar):
1. Image Generator
2. Video Generator
3. Spaces (covered in Lecture 10)

Image Generator:
- Select model: click "Auto" → "All Models" → choose (Flux 2.2 Pro is fast, 21s)
- Add style reference: click "Style" → these are pre-trained LoRAs
  (Pastel Cartoon, Oil Painting, etc.)
- Add character reference: upload a face image for character consistency
  (equivalent to IP Adapter in ComfyUI)
- Aspect ratio, resolution settings available
- Tip: Imagen 3 Pro = best quality for images; CDream 5 Lite = fastest

Style reference = what you now know as a LoRA.
You're selecting a pre-trained style adapter — FreePik is just hiding
the technical layer from you.

SECTION 3 — VIDEO GENERATOR
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Models available: Kling 2.5, Kling 3.0, CDance 2.0, LTX 2, VO3 Lite

Credit cost comparison (approximate):
- Kling 2.5: ~250 credits / video
- Kling 3.0 (5s, 720p, no audio): ~350 credits
- CDance 2.0: most expensive (~$2.5/video externally)
- Google VO3 Lite: cheapest option, uses fewest credits

Direct API vs. FreePik pricing:
- FreePik adds ~30-40% markup over direct API calls
  Example: Kling on FreePik = ~$0.20/sec vs. direct API = ~$0.14/sec
- For scale: use APIs directly. For learning/prototyping: FreePik fine.

Tips:
- Best image model on FreePik: Imagen 3 Pro (quality), CDream 5 Lite (speed)
- Best video models: Kling 3.0 (balance), CDance 2.0 (quality ceiling)
- For avatar/lip-sync: sync.ai (better facial expressions than HeyGen)
  but more expensive
- For voice cloning: 11Labs (audio only), CDance 2.0 (voice in video)

SECTION 5 — DATA PRIVACY NOTE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Important: Any image you upload as reference on FreePik is processed
on their servers. Any image given to Imagen 3 → goes to Google's servers.
Any reference fed to GPT models → OpenAI's servers.

This is why enterprises use ComfyUI locally:
Sensitive product data, face data, brand assets — they don't want
that leaving internal systems. Open-source local models = data stays yours.

This is a key reason why ComfyUI is still the industry standard
for any company with confidentiality requirements.
